Smart Home · Product Design · 2023

Transforming a traditional garage door company into a modern smart home ecosystem.

Project
B&D Smart Home
Role
Senior Product Designer
Industry
Smart Home Technology
Duration
2025

Reimagining B&D's mobile experience and design system to reposition the brand from a traditional garage door manufacturer into a connected smart home platform.

01 — Understanding the opportunity

Not a garage door app —
a brand repositioning.

B&D had built decades of trust among Australian homeowners, but its digital experience no longer reflected the expectations of modern connected-home users.

BeforeThe existing B&D app — capable, but device-led and visually dated.

The challenge wasn't redesigning a garage door app. It was changing how people perceive the brand.

Modern
Connected
Intelligent
Future-focused
02 — Design insight
Users don't want to control their garage.
They want confidence their home is secure.

This insight reframed the experience around home ownership, security, and peace of mind — not a remote control.

03 — From device control to home ownership

Reframing the centre
of the experience.

Before
Device-centric
A remote control in app form — one device, one action.
After
Home-centric
A connected home you own — organised around the property, not the hardware.
  • Property-focused navigation
  • 3D home representation
  • Smart device ecosystem
  • Stronger emotional connection
04 — Real-world behaviour

Built for one-handed use.

Reached for in the car, in the driveway, mid-task — fast, glanceable, one thumb.

  • Larger thumb-friendly controls
  • Vertical interaction pattern
  • Faster access while driving
  • Reduced interaction effort
05 — Dynamic garage controls

Turning physical movement
into intuitive digital behaviour.

The control adapts to the door's real state — so the next action is always obvious. System thinking, not feature explanation.

Closed → Open Opening → Stop Open → Close
06 — The welcome home moment

Designing emotion into a
functional experience.

When the garage door opens completely, the door itself disappears. The open state felt technically correct — but emotionally empty.

Empty state → arrivalA 3D dog turns a technically-correct open state into a moment worth feeling.
Outcome

Stakeholders immediately embraced the concept and identified future opportunities for personalisation — including pets, family avatars, and custom home scenes.

08 — Design system

A scalable foundation for
future connected products.

Built so new devices and scenes can join the ecosystem without re-designing the experience each time.

09 — Outcome

What changed.

Strong stakeholder approval
The concept was embraced immediately and championed internally as the brand's new direction.
Modernised brand perception
B&D shifted from a traditional manufacturer to a credible smart-home player.
Aligned with a smart-home vision
The experience now maps to where connected homes — and the business — are heading.
A scalable platform
A design system ready for future devices, scenes, and personalisation.
10 — Reflection

Designing beyond
the garage door.

This project reinforced that meaningful product experiences come from understanding the context behind user actions — not just the actions themselves.

By shifting the experience from garage control to home ownership, the product became more personal, memorable, and future-focused.
