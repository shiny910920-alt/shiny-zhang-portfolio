01 — Overview

Selling a Porsche,
the Porsche way.

Porsche Sell Direct is Porsche Australia's official platform for selling pre-owned vehicles directly to authorised Porsche dealerships. The platform enables Porsche owners to create vehicle listings, submit vehicle information, upload photos, and receive offers from approved Porsche Centres.

As Senior Product Designer, I focused on improving trust, simplifying the selling journey, and creating a premium digital experience aligned with Porsche's luxury brand standards — refining usability, information architecture, and the mobile experience across the platform.

02 — The Challenge

A high-trust
transaction.

Selling a luxury vehicle is one of the highest-value, highest-stakes transactions a customer can make online. The experience had to earn confidence at every step while feeling effortless — and unmistakably Porsche.

What it had to solve
  • Selling a luxury vehicle is a high-trust transaction — the platform must establish credibility immediately.
  • Users need confidence and transparency around what happens after they submit their vehicle.
  • The listing process needs to feel effortless, even when capturing detailed vehicle information and photos.
  • The digital experience must reflect Porsche's premium brand at every touchpoint.

06 — Reflection

Designing for
high-value trust.

Designing for a transaction of this value reframes what "good UX" means. When someone is selling a Porsche, the interface isn't competing for attention — it's earning confidence. Every interaction had to quietly reassure: your vehicle is understood, your information is safe, and a real Porsche Centre is on the other side.

The most interesting tension was balancing business objectives with user confidence. Dealerships need rich, accurate vehicle data to make competitive offers; owners want the lightest possible path to an outcome. The work lived in resolving that tension — structuring depth so it never felt heavy, and making transparency the default rather than an afterthought.

Working within an established global brand ecosystem meant designing with discipline. There was little room for invention for its own sake; the goal was to express Porsche's standards faithfully in a digital context — restraint, precision, and a premium feel achieved through detail rather than decoration. That constraint, more than anything, is what made the work satisfying.
