Consumer Rewards · AI Product Design · 2024–Present

Transforming a rewards platform into an intelligent consumer engagement ecosystem.

Project
Shping
Role
Senior Product Designer
Industry
Consumer Rewards Platform
Duration
2024 — Present

Reframing one of Australia's largest rewards platforms around value, motivation, and AI-driven personalisation — designing for both consumers and the brands behind them.

02 — Understanding the business

A two-sided
value engine.

Shping isn't a rewards app — it's a data exchange. Consumers are paid for their attention and purchasing behaviour; brands pay for the insight that behaviour generates. Designing it well means serving both sides of that relationship at once.

Consumers
  • Earn through receipt scanning, reviews, surveys & cashback
  • ~4,000 daily active users — household shoppers, value-conscious
  • Australians aged 30–60, managing real grocery budgets
Give: purchasing & behavioural data
SHPING
←  →
The exchange layer
value · trust · insight
Brands
  • Retailers, manufacturers & supermarkets
  • Buy consumer insight, campaigns & sponsored content
  • Reach verified, real-purchase audiences
Give: rewards, campaigns & revenue
INPUT
Behaviour
Scans, reviews, surveys and brand interactions capture genuine purchasing signals.
EXCHANGE
Rewards
Consumers are compensated in real, redeemable value for the data they share.
OUTPUT
Insight
Aggregated behaviour becomes consumer-insight reporting for brands.
REVENUE
Campaigns
Insight powers advertising, sponsored content and targeted brand campaigns.
03 — The challenge

Rewarding users
isn't the same as
engaging them.

The platform worked — it paid people. But over years of feature accumulation it had grown dense and hard to navigate, and the reward systems became difficult to reason about. It successfully rewarded users yet struggled to keep them coming back.

This was never really an interface problem. It was a behaviour problem.

What users told us
  • Too much information on every screen
  • An outdated, dated user experience
  • Navigation that was hard to move through
  • Reward systems that were confusing to understand
  • Little motivation to engage beyond a one-off reward
Effort required
>
Perceived reward

When the effort feels greater than the payoff, engagement quietly erodes — no matter how generous the rewards actually are.

04 — User insights

Three truths that
reframed the product.

01
Motivation · Value perception
Users don't think in points.
They think in money.
How much is this actually worth?

The product led with abstract point balances, but users translated everything back into dollars. By foregrounding points instead of value, the experience quietly weakened the very motivation it was trying to create.

02
Cognitive load · Decision-making
Users wanted guidance —
not more choices.

People struggled to know which actions actually created rewards, which task to do next, and how to maximise earnings. The system pushed too much decision-making onto the user. Freedom had become friction.

03
Emotion · Reward psychology
Rewards were earned,
but never felt.

Users liked receiving rewards, yet completing an activity produced little emotional payoff. Reward moments were transactional — a number changed somewhere. Nothing about it felt like an achievement worth repeating.

05 — Product strategy

Five pillars,
one behaviour goal.

Not a redesign brief — a product strategy. Each pillar targets a specific point where motivation was breaking down, and together they shift Shping from a transactional rewards tool toward an engagement ecosystem.

STRATEGY 01
Make rewards feel real
Prioritise wallet value and monetary outcomes over abstract point balances. Speak in the currency users already think in — dollars, not points.
STRATEGY 02
Reduce onboarding friction
Let users experience value before asking for commitment. Defer secondary data collection to later, once trust and momentum exist.
STRATEGY 03
Create stronger behaviour loops
Transform Daily Tasks into a guided engagement journey. Users should never hunt for opportunities — the platform should guide the behaviour.
STRATEGY 04
Increase emotional reward
Celebrate achievements, visualise progress, and reinforce participation through behavioural psychology — make earning feel like winning.
STRATEGY 05 · THE INFLECTION POINT
Personalise discovery through AI
Use purchase history and behavioural patterns to surface relevant products, cashback opportunities, insights and recommendations — reducing decision fatigue and increasing relevance. This is where Shping stops being a rewards app and starts becoming an intelligent system.
06 — Dashboard transformation

From a wall of
information to a
guided system.

The old dashboard asked users to be their own product manager — to scan everything and decide what mattered. The new dashboard makes that decision for them.

Designing for hierarchy, not density

Every element earned its place against one question: does this help the user understand their value, or their next action? Everything else moved down, out, or away.

  • Reward value surfaced first — money before points
  • A clear visual hierarchy that resolves "what now?" instantly
  • Reward visibility designed to motivate, not just inform
  • Cognitive load reduced by removing competing priorities
07 — Behaviour design & daily tasks

Designing the loop,
not just the screen.

Daily Tasks was the highest-leverage surface in the product. Reframing it from a list of chores into a designed habit loop is where engagement is actually won or lost.

CUE
Guided prompt
The platform proactively surfaces the next best action — the user never has to go looking for it.
ACTION
Low-friction task
Scan, review, answer — each task is small, clear, and obviously connected to a reward.
REWARD
Felt payoff
Value lands immediately and emotionally — progress is celebrated and made visible.
INVESTMENT
Visible progress
Streaks and accumulated value give the user something to protect — and a reason to return tomorrow.
Motivation system

Turning chores into a streak worth keeping.

The shift was from "here are things you could do" to "here is your next move." Guided actions remove the search cost; visible momentum supplies the motivation.

  • Habit loop: cue → action → reward → investment
  • Guided next-best-action replaces open-ended menus
  • Progress visualisation creates loss-averse momentum
  • Celebration moments convert completion into emotion
◇ AI PRODUCT DESIGN
08 — AI personalisation

Meet Shping AI.

The strategic inflection point — and the part of this project that defines my move into AI product design. Personalisation reframes the entire experience from "browse everything" to "here's what matters to you."

Every user shops differently. The product should too — driven by behaviour, not by a one-size-fits-all feed.
RECOMMENDATION
Behaviour-driven discovery
Purchase history and interaction patterns drive what each user sees — products, offers and content ranked by genuine relevance rather than recency.
RELEVANCE
Purchase-based matching
Cashback and campaigns surface against what someone actually buys — turning generic offers into personally meaningful value.
COGNITION
Reduced decision fatigue
By deciding what to show, the system removes the effort that was eroding engagement — the platform now does the searching, so the user doesn't have to.

Positioning AI here isn't a feature bolt-on — it's a product thesis. The same behavioural data that powers Shping's business model becomes the engine for a personalised consumer experience. The two-sided ecosystem and the AI layer are the same system, seen from two directions.

09 — Stories feature

Participation beyond the transaction.

Scanning and surveys are extractive by nature — the user gives, the platform takes. Stories opens a second mode of participation: one where users contribute, express, and belong.

  • Content creation that earns beyond pure transactions
  • Community engagement that deepens platform attachment
  • AI-assisted content opportunities that lower the effort to contribute
  • A richer signal for brands than receipts alone can provide

CAMPAIGNS

CONSUMERS

PRODUCTS

11 — Design system

A system built
to move faster.

Not a component library for its own sake — infrastructure for velocity. With consumer app, marketing experience and B2B dashboard all evolving in parallel, the system is what keeps them coherent.

SCALABILITY
One language, many surfaces
Shared foundations stretch cleanly from a consumer app to a B2B analytics platform without fragmenting the brand.
CONSISTENCY
Coherence under change
As features ship continuously, the system keeps the experience feeling like one product rather than many bolt-ons.
VELOCITY
Faster product development
Reusable patterns turn new features from blank-canvas problems into assembly — shipping value to users sooner.
ALIGNMENT
Cross-platform alignment
Designers and engineers share one source of truth across consumer and brand-facing products.
12 — Outcomes

What changed.

Qualitative shifts in how the product creates — and communicates — value, for both sides of the ecosystem.

Clearer reward understanding
Value is expressed in money, not abstract points — users finally know what their effort is worth.
Improved engagement opportunities
Guided Daily Tasks replace open-ended searching with a clear, repeatable path to value.
Stronger motivation
Behavioural psychology and celebration turn completion into emotion — earning feels like winning.
Better product discoverability
AI personalisation surfaces relevant products and offers, cutting decision fatigue.
A more scalable ecosystem
A shared design system and a unified data-to-AI thesis position Shping to grow on both the consumer and brand sides without re-architecting the experience each time.
13 — Reflection

Designing systems
that pay both sides.

Shping reframed how I think about product design. The hardest problem here wasn't the interface — it was behaviour. People weren't disengaging because the screens were ugly; they were disengaging because the effort outweighed the felt reward. Solving that meant designing motivation, not layouts.

It also sharpened my belief in ecosystem thinking. A consumer rewards platform only works if both sides win — users feel real value, and brands receive real insight. Every decision had to hold both truths at once. The best product strategy I could offer wasn't a feature; it was alignment between the two.

Most of all, this project is where my practice tips from product design toward AI product design. The same behavioural data that funds the business is the raw material for personalisation. Once you see that, AI stops being a feature and becomes the operating logic of the product — deciding what's relevant, reducing decision fatigue, and making the experience adapt to each person.

The lesson I'm carrying forward: the strongest systems don't choose between user value and business value — they convert one into the other. Design's job is to make that exchange feel effortless, motivating, and, increasingly, intelligent.
